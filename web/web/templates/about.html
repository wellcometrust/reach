About Reach

Reach was developed to help researchers, policy makers and funders to better understand how scientific research is used in policy.

What is the focus of Reach?

Reach was developed to improve understanding of the use of research in policy. We have chosen organisations that are influential health policy voices in the UK or at the global level.

What are the policy sources?

We source policy documents from UNICEF, Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF), National Institute of Clinical Excellence (NICE), the World Health Organisation (WHO), the UK government and the UK parliament. The documents include Reports, Health Technology Assessments, guidelines and position papers. If there are any sources you think are missing, please get in touch with the team.

What are the scientific publications?

We use the EPMC database to cross-reference the references that Reach finds in policy documents. If a paper is not in EPMC then it will not be reported by Reach even if it has been cited. Click here to find more about How Reach Works.

Open Source

We are committed to making Reach an open source tool. Our entire code can be found on Github and our open API gives you access to the data that powers Reach.

Accuracy

Reach is powered by cutting edge machine learning techniques applied by the data scientists in Wellcome Data Labs. Machine learning is not 100% accurate because it is based on probability, but we are constantly working on Reach to improve its accuracy. If something doesn’t look quite right, please get in touch with the team.

Transparency

We are committed to supporting responsible data innovation and product development. If you are interested in following our work, we publish regular blogs on our Medium channel.

About Wellcome Data Labs

Wellcome Data Labs exists to enable responsible, data-driven decision making at Wellcome and in the wider research cuommunity. We build products, support data innovation in health and research, and blend technology social sciences approaches in our work.
